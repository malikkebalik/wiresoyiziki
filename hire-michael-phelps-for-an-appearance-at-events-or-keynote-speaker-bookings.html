The famed American swimmer Michael Phelps is regarded as one of the greatest Olympians of all time, if not the very best. His distinguished career has been garnished with an astounding total of 28 medals, including an Olympic gold medal record-breaking 23 golds. It's impressive that he won 13 gold medals in the Olympics in individual competitions and an additional 16 medals overall in individual competitions. At the Beijing Games, he broke Mark Spitz's record of seven victories in a single Olympics by winning an unprecedented eight gold medals, which proved to be a turning point in his Olympic career.

Phelps' genius was on display at the Athens Summer Olympics, where he won eight medals—six gold and two bronze—for his efforts. He won six additional medals in London, including four gold and two silver. Additionally, he won six medals in his most recent participation at the Rio de Janeiro Summer Olympics, including an amazing five gold and one silver.

Michael Phelps is a master of many swimming strokes; his accomplishments in the butterfly, individual medley, freestyle, and backstroke are particularly noteworthy. His accomplishments have not gone unnoticed as evidenced by the fact that Sports Illustrated named him Sportsman of the Year, and he has also won a number of major honors such as World Swimmer of the Year, American Swimmer of the Year, and FINA Swimmer of the Year.

In addition swimming his outstanding athletic career, Phelps has pursued philanthropy and mental health awareness. He founded the Michael Phelps Foundation, which focuses on empowering people through swimming, and joined the board of Medibio, a business that deals with mental health issues.

Phelps's journey began when he was a young child and discovered swimming as a way to release his frustration. He was identified as having ADHD, but he rapidly discovered a love for the game and showed incredible aptitude at a young age..
