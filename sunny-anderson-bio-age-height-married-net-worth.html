Who is Sunny Anderso? 

Sunny Anderson is an American celebrity chef, cookbook author, and television personality. 

She is best known for hosting cooking shows on the Food Network like Cooking for Real and How’d That Get on My Plate. And The Kitchen.

 Sunny was born in Oklahoma in 1975 and grew up travelling with her family as her father was in the US Army. 

She joined the US Air Force after high school and later worked as a radio host before pursuing her passion for cooking professionally. 

Some key facts about Sunny include that she was diagnosed with ulcerative colitis at a young age, owns her own catering business, and has authored a best-selling cookbook. 

Sunny aims to make home cooking easy, affordable and enjoyable for everyone through her shows and recipes.

Sunny Anderson Career 

Sunny Anderson has had an interesting career spanning several fields. She began as a radio host for the US Air Force from 1993 to 1997. 

After leaving the Air Force, she worked as a radio DJ in different cities from 1995 to 2001.

In 2001, she moved to New York and worked as a radio host for HOT 97 FM. She also started her own catering business, Sunny’s Delicious Dishes, from 2003 to 2005.

Sunny got her big break in 2005 when she debuted on the Food Network show Emeril Live. 

In 2008, she got her show called How’d That Get On My Plate?. Since then, she has hosted several successful shows on Food Network like Cooking For Real, Gotta Get It and Home Made in America.

She is one of the co-hosts of the popular Food Network series The Kitchen, along with other celebrity chefs. 

Sunny has also authored a successful cookbook titled Sunny’s Kitchen: Easy Food for Real Life, a New York Times bestseller.

Throughout her career spanning several decades, Sunny has established herself as a popular chef and TV personality known for her fun and lively hosting style on food shows.

Sunny Anderson Mac and Cheese Recipe

Here are the simple steps for Sunny Anderson’s creamy 5-cheese mac and cheese recipe:

  • Butter a baking dish and preheat oven to 375°F.
  • Mix flour, paprika, mustard, garlic, scallions, hot sauce, and cream cheese in a bowl.
  • Gradually add heavy cream and pasta water while whisking.
  • Add shredded Cheddar, Gouda, mozzarella and Pecorino Romano cheese.
  • Cook elbow pasta al dente and mix into the cheese sauce.
  • Pour into the baking dish and top with more Cheddar and mozzarella.
  • Bake for 35-40 minutes until bubbly and golden brown.
  • Remove from the oven and enjoy this cheesy mac and cheese!

    • Sunny’s recipe uses 5 cheeses to make it creamy and flavorful. The baking makes it even more delicious!
